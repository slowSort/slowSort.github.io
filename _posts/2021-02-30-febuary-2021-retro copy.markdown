Febuary was an interesting month. There was a lot of good progress towards our goal, but it's unclear if we're on the right path at all with our product.

## Last Month’s Goals

**Send 10 emails to gauge interest in a new product: B**\
By the last day of the month we had drafted and then sent 3 emails to zero waste buisnesses using WooCommerce. Our idea was to start small and maybe the feedback we got would make us tailor our emails for the next batch. After no responses we decided perhaps we'd have to send out a lot more than we thought, and by the day I'm writing this (a week late) we have sent out around 10-15.

**Build a Woocommerce plugin: B**\
This was something we definitely did. Before this neither me nor my partner have ever built a Woocommerce plugin, or any kind of Wordpress plugin. What we've got is not pretty, and would take another 5-10 hours of development to get to a polished state, but we're confident we could do it no problem. I give it a B only due to it not being in a sellable state as of this very moment.

## Zero waste sell by weight plugin

Our idea was fairly simple. Zero waste stores have online versions, a lot of them built in Woocommerce. One of the advantages of Zero waste stores is that you can buy exactly how much you need. If you want only 40 grams of flour, you can get it. This is not possible easily online, as most stores are set up to sell fixed quantities of things. We figured zero waste stores might pay £5-10 a month for a plugin that allows their shoppers to do this.

We decided to build the app first, then send out pre-sale emails. In retrospect I think this was a mistake. Our thinking was "why try and sell something we don't know if we can build". What I am thinking now is "why did we try to build something we did not know we could sell?". While we learned a lot about making Woocommerce plugins (definitely not a waste of time), we only started learning about our product when we started sending emails and getting responses from people. One particularly useful person told us that they were actually moving from selling by weight to selling in fixed quantities, because it was a nightmare to have to measure out each delivery individually, and in 50g increments she could pre-pack ingredients. How's that for blowing our assumptions out the water! The idea's not over yet, and it's clear certain stores look for ways to do this, but next time we need to validate an idea, we'll probably start by sending out emails to the people we'd be trying to make our customers.

There's one really useful thing I've learned this month. It's one thing to hear someone give good advice in a talk, and a completely different thing to go implement it straight away. There's many a micro buisness owner who talked about starting with emails, but there were also a few who said building first is a good idea. At the end of the day, some lessons can only be internalised by experience. I'm sure there's going to be tonnes of times I'll do something that other successful people have been preaching about for ages, and wish I did it sooner. For now I'm just happy I'm on the path, making my own mistakes, learning my own lessons.

## March goal

This goal marks a turning point for us. If we're unable to get 3 people saying, "sure I'd buy it" from our list of over 200 zero-waste buisnesses in the UK, it's unclear if we're really solving a problem that people have. We've decided to do our best to market the idea to as many people as will listen, but we're ready to move on if it's clear this is not something we can make at least takeout money from.
