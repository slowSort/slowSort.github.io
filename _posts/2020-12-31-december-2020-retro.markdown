I've been meaning to start a blog for a long time, and I've finally got around to it! To help get myself off to a good 2021 (this year has been as interesting for me as it has for you, I'm sure) I've decided to try start a monthly retrospective. I hope this can get me to think about my future in month size chunks, and set me up to grow myself in all the ways I want.

I've been tracking my time for at least a month now on my major projects using [toggle.com], so I'll base some of my monthly goals off of the information I've got from there.

![toggl screenshot](/assets/images/togglDec2020.png)

## Fitness

This includes weightlifting and BJJ. Ideally I want to be driving down this number as low as I can so I have more time for other things. BJJ on the other hand is something I actually want to increase (pretty silly of me to track them together!). This next month I'll track it differently and see what I can do. For now my goals are just to conservatively make my workouts shorter and my BJJ sessions longer

**Goal 1: at least 12 hours of BJJ**\
**Goal 2: at most 20 hours of Weightlifting**

## Polish

Polish is a big deal to me, my partner Abi is from Poland, and is fluent in it and English. I used furlough during the pandemic to start learning, and I use a mass immersion approach (I listen and read lot in Polish). I already do a lot here, and I don't want to set any arbritrary goals, so for now I'll just try to keep up the work I'm doing. If anyone is interested in learning about immersion learning I can't recommend [this website] enough.

**Goal 3: 3 hours every single day of Polish immersion**

## Programming + "Without Project"

I've been playing about with a few solo projects this year.

- I made a site based on the Zettelkasten note taking method to practice with Redux and React Testing Library
- I made an Optical Character Recognition site using [tessaract] to try help me translate text from Anime that was translated into Polish
- I got back into Hackerrank and tried to brush up on my Java

While these have been fun, they never really motivated me too much and all ended up burning out. I learned a lot, but did not really have anything solid at the end or a strong habit of working on them in my free time. This has changed completely as me and partner have recently started working on trying to release an actual product. It's so motivating to be working on trying to build something real, and we're super excited to try and realise our dreams of owning a small business that pays the bills. We're a long way away but for now, we're super motivated to start our journey and start learning right away.

**Goal 4: Launch the MVP website of our very first buisness idea**

I think that's enough for now. I want to keep these posts small and useful for myself. I'm sure I'll iterate on the format (think of this as a MVP blog post!) and can already think of a whole bunch of things I could add or talk about in the future. I guess I'm just excited for the new year. If anyone is reading this, all the best, and I hope you're feeling motivated to work on your goals too.
